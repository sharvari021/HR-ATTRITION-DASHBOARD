# HR Employee Attrition Dashboard 📊

## Overview
An interactive HR Attrition Dashboard built using **Power BI**
as part of the **SkillCraft Technology Data Analyst Internship - Task 03**.

## Objective
To analyze why employees are leaving the company by building
an interactive dashboard that allows filtering by:
- Department
- Age Group
- Gender
- Job Role
- Education Field

## Key Questions Answered
- Why are employees leaving?
- Which department has highest attrition?
- Does salary affect attrition?
- Does job satisfaction affect attrition?
- Which age group leaves the most?

## Dashboard Features
- KPI Cards — Total Employees, Attrition Count, 
  Attrition Rate%, Active Employees
- Donut Chart — Attrition by Department
- Bar Chart — Attrition by Age Group
- Bar Chart — Attrition by Job Role
- Column Chart — Attrition by Salary Slab
- Column Chart — Attrition Rate by Job Satisfaction
- Line Chart — Attrition by Years at Company
- 5 Interactive Slicers for filtering

## Key Insights
- Employees earning Upto 5K salary have highest attrition (150+)
- Age group 26-35 has the highest attrition count
- Low job satisfaction (rating 1) leads to highest attrition rate
- Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives leave the most
- Most attrition happens in first 2 years at company

## Tools Used
- Power BI Desktop
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- IBM HR Analytics Dataset (Kaggle)

## Dataset
IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance
Source: Kaggle
Records: 1,470 employees
